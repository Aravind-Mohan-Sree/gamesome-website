THE FATHER OF VIDEO GAMES...

Ralph Baer

For video games, that person was Ralph Baer. Long considered the Father of the Video Game, Baer’s curiosity and persistence in the 1960s made possible the development and commercialization of the interactive video games and modern consoles we know and love today. One of the earliest playable prototypes he created was a game called "Brown Box," which eventually evolved into the Magnavox Odyssey. The first commercial video game released for the Magnavox Odyssey was a simple table tennis game called "Table Tennis" or "Ping-Pong," which was similar to the classic game Pong. This game, along with several others released for the Odyssey, laid the foundation for the video game industry as we know it today.

DID YOU KNOW?

The first video game ever made was called "Spacewar!" It was developed in 1962 by Steve Russell and ran on the PDP-1 (Programmed Data Processor-1) computer.

The iconic character Pac-Man was originally called "Puck-Man" when it was first released in Japan. The name was changed when it was brought to the United States to prevent vandals from altering the arcade cabinets to say a profanity.

The best-selling video game franchise of all time is "Mario," created by Nintendo. With games spanning various genres and platforms, Mario has sold over 688 million units worldwide.

The first Easter egg in a video game was found in the game "Adventure" for the Atari 2600. Programmer Warren Robinett hid his name within the game as a secret room, which players could only access by performing a series of complex maneuvers.

"Duke Nukem Forever" holds the record for the longest development time of any video game. It was in development for over 15 years before it was finally released in 2011.

The blocky graphics of Minecraft were originally due to technical limitations but became iconic for the game. Markus Persson, the creator of Minecraft, designed the game with simple graphics to focus more on gameplay mechanics.
